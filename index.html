Gym Etiquette Tip/Demand Of The Week

If It's Not A Bumper Plate, DO NOT DROP IT

In the first in our electrifying series Gym Etiquette Tip/Demand Of The Week, we'd like to remind you to not drop any weights that are not bumper plates.

If you have enough experience in the gym to lift weights so heavy that you have to drop them, you also have enough experience to know you shouldn't drop weights that aren't designed for dropping. Not only is it insanely loud, it also ruins the bars and the weights (and the floor, and the ceiling, and and and...). So please just don't do it.

As you might imagine, this holds doubly true on the Roof. We have had lights literally fall out of the ceiling onto people from dropping heavy weights on the Roof.

To further help with this, we've ordered drop pads you can use to put your enormous weights on and further reduce the sound and damage to equipment. Please use them!
